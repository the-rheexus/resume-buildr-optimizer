### 5.4 Multi-lane bullets (angle the same work for two lanes)
 
Same underlying work, three different bullets depending on target lane.
 
**The work:** Led an internal pricing analysis that recommended a SKU repackage.
 
- Strategy angle: Led pricing strategy analysis across 4 product SKUs in $80M portfolio; built bottom-up willingness-to-pay model, presented repackage recommendation to executive committee, repackage launched and lifted ARPU 14% in two quarters.
- PM angle: Owned SKU repackage as cross-functional PM lead; collaborated with sales, finance, and engineering to redesign packaging and pricing pages; launched in 6 weeks and lifted self-serve conversion 22% with no change to ARR per customer.
- Analytics angle: Built willingness-to-pay model in Python using customer survey data (n=1,200) and historical billing data; quantified price elasticity across 4 SKUs and 3 customer segments; analysis informed repackage that lifted ARPU 14% with negligible churn impact.
The underlying facts are the same. The framing, verb choice, and emphasis change.

## 6. Quick reference: bullet quality test
 
Before accepting any bullet on the resume, it should pass all of these:
 
1. Does it start with a strong action verb? (not "responsible for," "helped," "worked on")
2. Does the reader know what problem or context the work addressed?
3. Does the bullet name a specific action the candidate took?
4. Is there a quantified business outcome or, at minimum, a clear scope and scale?
5. Does the language match the target lane (see sections 2.3, 3.3, 4.3)?
6. Could the candidate defend this bullet in detail under interview pressure?
If any answer is no, rewrite.
