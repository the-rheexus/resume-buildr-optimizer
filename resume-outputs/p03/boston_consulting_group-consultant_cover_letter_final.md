```
Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for the Consultant, Experienced Hire role at Boston Consulting Group. As
a Carlson MBA brand and strategy consultant, I led the analysis and executive
presentation behind a Little Tikes brand-architecture refresh. I delivered three
data-driven recommendations to CMO- and VP-level stakeholders on a six-person team.
This is the kind of cross-functional work, with colleagues from many backgrounds and
disciplines, that BCG asks its consultants to lead.

For that Little Tikes engagement, I analyzed a consumer survey of 700 responses,
retained 500 after cleaning, and turned the results into three recommendations that
were mostly adopted for the brand-architecture refresh. I ran a parallel engagement
for the University of Minnesota's School of Nursing DNP program, benchmarking peer
programs and delivering positioning recommendations that were acted on as part of
optimizing the program's enrollment funnel. Both engagements meant translating
ambiguous research findings into recommendations a non-specialist executive audience
could put into practice immediately. That is also the wide range of clients and
projects that BCG's casework demands, across industries as different as
pharmaceutical and consumer goods.

BCG describes its work as cases that reshape business, government, and society,
backed by mentors and structured learning toward an eventual practice-area
specialization. That is what draws me to this role over a narrower, single-industry
seat. My MBA is complete as of May 2026, well ahead of any realistic start date. I am
comfortable with BCG's client-site travel and extended-hours model, and specifically
open to relocating to the Chicago, San Diego, or Washington, D.C. office.

I would welcome a conversation about how my survey-driven strategy work could support
BCG's consulting teams. Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
Joshua Ekong
```
