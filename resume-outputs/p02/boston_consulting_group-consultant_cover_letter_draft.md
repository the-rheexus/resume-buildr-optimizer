Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for the Consultant, Experienced Hire role at Boston Consulting Group. As
a Carlson MBA brand and strategy consultant, I led the analysis and executive
presentation behind a Little Tikes brand-architecture refresh, delivering three
data-driven recommendations to CMO- and VP-level stakeholders on a six-person team, the
kind of cross-functional work with colleagues from many backgrounds and disciplines
that BCG asks its consultants to lead.

For that Little Tikes engagement, I led the analysis of a consumer survey, 700
responses collected and 500 retained after cleaning, and turned it into three
recommendations that were substantially adopted for the brand-architecture refresh. I
ran a parallel engagement for the University of Minnesota's School of Nursing DNP
program, benchmarking peer programs and delivering positioning recommendations that
were adopted as part of optimizing the program's enrollment funnel. Both engagements
meant translating ambiguous, cross-functional research into recommendations a
non-specialist executive audience could act on immediately, the same wide range of
clients and projects BCG's casework demands across industries as different as
pharmaceutical and consumer goods.

BCG's description of cases that reshape business, government, and society, backed by
mentors and structured learning toward an eventual practice-area specialization, is
what draws me to this role over a narrower, single-industry seat. My MBA is complete as
of May 2026, well ahead of any realistic start date, and I'm comfortable with BCG's
client-site travel and extended-hours model. I'm specifically open to relocating to the
Chicago, San Diego, or Washington, D.C. office.

I would welcome a conversation about how my survey-driven strategy work could support
BCG's consulting teams. Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,
Joshua Ekong
